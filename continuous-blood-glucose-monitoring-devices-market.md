<p><p>Continuous Blood Glucose Monitoring (CBGM) devices are categorized into two main types: wearable and implantable. Wearable devices, such as patches or wristbands, are designed for easy application on the skin's surface, allowing for real-time glucose tracking without invasive procedures. In contrast, implantable devices are inserted beneath the skin to provide long-term monitoring, offering greater accuracy and reduced need for frequent replacements. Both types enhance diabetes management by delivering continuous data, enabling users to make informed health decisions.</p></p>
